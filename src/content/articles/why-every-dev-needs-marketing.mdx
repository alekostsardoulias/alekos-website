# Why Every Developer Should Learn Marketing Basics

There is a quiet resentment in tech culture toward marketing. Developers see it as fluff, manipulation, or at best a necessary evil. This is a mistake — and one that costs engineers career growth, influence, and the satisfaction of building things people actually use.

## Marketing Is Just Communication at Scale

Strip away the buzzwords and marketing is simple: understanding who needs what you build and telling them about it clearly. That is it. The same clarity you apply to naming variables and writing documentation applies to explaining your product. A well-crafted landing page and a well-designed API have more in common than you might think — both reduce cognitive load for the person on the other side.

## The Career Advantage

Developers who can market themselves and their work advance faster. They write better READMEs that get their open-source projects adopted. They can pitch ideas to stakeholders without relying on someone else to translate. They build side projects that actually get users instead of sitting in a GitHub graveyard.

You do not need an MBA. Read a book on positioning — Al Ries and Jack Trout's classic is still the best. Learn the basics of copywriting. Pay attention to how products you love talk about themselves. Notice what made you click, sign up, or buy.

The developers I know who earn the most and have the most autonomy are not necessarily the best coders. They are the ones who can build something and then explain why it matters. That second skill is marketing — and it is learnable.
