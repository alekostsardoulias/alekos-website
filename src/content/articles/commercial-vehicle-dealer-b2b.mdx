A European logistics company specializing in the sale of new and pre-owned commercial trucks had a long-standing reputation built on decades of face-to-face relationships and trade show networking. Their physical showrooms and service centers were well-regarded, yet their digital sales infrastructure lagged dangerously behind a rapidly changing market. Fleet managers and logistics directors were increasingly starting their purchasing journeys online, researching models, comparing specifications, and requesting quotes through web forms and LinkedIn. The company's existing website was a static, decade-old catalogue of vehicles with no search filters, no lead capture forms, and no mobile optimization. Paid advertising was non-existent. Social media presence was limited to an occasionally updated LinkedIn page with product photos and no engagement strategy. The consequence was clear: the company was invisible at the most critical moment of the modern B2B buyer's journey, leaving a growing volume of high-intent demand to competitors who had invested in their digital storefronts.

The core problem was not the quality of the inventory or the expertise of the sales team. It was the absence of a digital engine that could attract qualified buyers, convert their interest into actionable leads, and nurture them systematically. The company needed a new website that functioned as a 24-hour sales representative, a paid media strategy that captured in-market buyers across search and social platforms, and a social media framework that built brand authority and trust before a handshake ever took place.

## Phase 1: A High-Performance Website Built for Lead Conversion

The foundation of the entire project was a completely new website, designed from the ground up to serve the needs of commercial fleet buyers. Rather than using a generic template, I built a custom WordPress solution with a headless frontend layer powered by Next.js for static generation and blazing-fast load times. This architecture allowed the vehicle inventory to be managed through a familiar admin panel while delivering a modern, app-like browsing experience to the end user.

The vehicle catalogue was the centerpiece. Each truck listing was a dynamic page pulling from a custom post type enriched with structured data fields: manufacturer, model, year, mileage, engine specifications, gross vehicle weight, Euro emission standard, and price. A robust filtering system was implemented using faceted search, enabling buyers to narrow down hundreds of vehicles by multiple criteria simultaneously without page reloads. Every vehicle page was optimized for search engines with auto-generated meta tags, schema markup for vehicle inventory, and semantic HTML. High-resolution image galleries and downloadable spec sheets were standard.

Lead capture was woven into the fabric of the browsing experience, not bolted on as an afterthought. Contextual call-to-action buttons appeared on every vehicle page: "Request a Quote," "Book a Test Drive," "Download Full Specifications." These actions opened lightweight, multi-step forms that fed directly into a HubSpot CRM via its API. Each submission was enriched with the vehicle ID and the visitor's browsing history, giving the sales team full context before they made the first call. A finance calculator was integrated on each listing, allowing buyers to estimate monthly lease payments and submit a financing inquiry directly. The website also featured a trade-in valuation tool, capturing sellers looking to upgrade their fleet.

Technical performance was monitored rigorously. Google Analytics 4 and Google Tag Manager were configured to track micro-conversions such as video views, spec sheet downloads, and time spent on inventory pages. These events were later used as signals for paid campaign optimization and lead scoring. The site was hosted on a cloud platform with a content delivery network, ensuring fast load times across the company's key European markets. Multilingual support for English, German, and French was built into the CMS, recognizing that fleet buyers often operate across borders.

## Phase 2: Paid Advertising Campaigns Across Search and Social

With a conversion-ready website in place, the next phase was to generate qualified traffic at scale. I designed and managed paid campaigns across Google Ads and LinkedIn Ads, each platform serving a distinct role in the buyer's journey.

On Google Ads, the strategy was built around high-intent search queries. I conducted extensive keyword research to identify terms that commercial fleet buyers use when actively looking to purchase: specific model numbers, "buy used Mercedes Actros," "refrigerated truck for sale Europe," "fleet upgrade 4x2 tractor unit." Campaigns were segmented tightly by vehicle type, new versus used, and geographic market. Each ad group pointed to a relevant landing page, often a pre-filtered inventory view, rather than the homepage. Ad copy highlighted the company's unique selling propositions: pan-European delivery, manufacturer-certified inspections, flexible financing, and multi-brand inventory. Sitelink extensions directed users to finance calculators and trade-in forms, increasing the surface area for clicks.

Conversion tracking was the backbone of campaign management. Every quote request, finance application, and phone call from a Google Ads click was measured and fed back into the platform's bidding algorithm. I implemented enhanced conversions to improve attribution accuracy. A remarketing strategy was layered on top, using display and YouTube ads to re-engage visitors who had browsed specific vehicles but not converted. These ads featured the exact trucks they had viewed, pulling images and prices dynamically from the website's inventory feed.

On LinkedIn Ads, the objective was to reach decision-makers who were not yet actively searching but fit the ideal customer profile. I built campaigns targeting fleet managers, logistics directors, and procurement officers at mid-sized to large transportation companies across Europe. Ad formats included sponsored content that drove to an educational blog series on fleet total cost of ownership and lead generation forms pre-filled with LinkedIn profile data. The messaging focused on business outcomes: reducing downtime, meeting sustainability targets with Euro 6 vehicles, and optimizing fleet lifecycle costs. The combination of search ads capturing bottom-of-funnel demand and LinkedIn ads generating top-of-funnel awareness created a balanced pipeline that the sales team could work effectively.

Budget allocation was continuously optimized. Campaigns with a lower cost per qualified lead received incremental budget, while underperformers were paused or restructured. A monthly performance dashboard, built in Looker Studio, automatically pulled data from Google Ads, LinkedIn, and HubSpot to give leadership a clear, unified view of cost per lead, lead-to-opportunity rate, and customer acquisition cost by channel.

## Phase 3: Social Media Marketing and Automation

The social media presence required a fundamental repositioning. The company's LinkedIn page was transformed from a sporadic photo album into a strategic content hub designed to build authority in the commercial vehicle space. I developed a content strategy anchored in the pain points and aspirations of fleet operators: vehicle maintenance best practices, regulatory updates on emissions standards, financial tips for fleet renewal, and behind-the-scenes looks at vehicle inspections and refurbishment processes.

A structured editorial calendar was created, with weekly themes mapped to the sales team's inventory priorities. For example, a month focused on refrigerated transport would feature articles on temperature-controlled logistics, customer testimonials from food distributors, and spotlight posts on available reefer trucks. The content mix included original written articles posted to the company's blog and shared as LinkedIn articles, short video walkarounds of newly arrived vehicles filmed by the sales team, and curated industry news with expert commentary.

Automation was introduced to ensure consistency without burdening the small marketing team. I configured Buffer's API to connect with the editorial calendar, scheduling posts across LinkedIn and Facebook at optimal times based on audience activity data. Visual assets were created using Canva templates that maintained a cohesive brand identity, with vehicle photos dynamically inserted when possible. An employee advocacy workflow was set up, providing sales representatives with pre-written posts they could share to their personal LinkedIn networks with a single tap, amplifying reach organically.

Engagement was not left to chance. I set up social listening for relevant keywords and competitor mentions, flagging conversations where the company could add value. Incoming messages and comments were routed to a shared team inbox with automated categorization, ensuring that inquiries about specific vehicles were directed to the responsible salesperson within hours. A weekly automated report summarized engagement metrics, follower growth, and top-performing posts, giving the team actionable insights without manual data crunching.

## Tools and Methods Summary

- **Next.js + WordPress Headless CMS**: For a fast, SEO-optimized website with a dynamic vehicle catalogue, advanced filtering, and structured data.
- **HubSpot CRM**: Central lead database with API integration for all website forms, tracking lead source, vehicle interest, and browsing history.
- **Google Analytics 4 & Google Tag Manager**: Event-based tracking of micro and macro conversions, enhanced conversions for ad optimization.
- **Google Ads**: Search campaigns segmented by vehicle type and market, dynamic remarketing with inventory feed integration, call tracking.
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager**: Sponsored content and lead generation ads targeting fleet decision-makers by job title, industry, and company size.
- **Buffer API**: Cross-platform social media scheduling with analytics and engagement tracking.
- **Canva API**: Branded template generation for consistent visual assets.
- **Looker Studio**: Automated cross-channel performance dashboards blending ad, website, and CRM data.
- **Cloud hosting with CDN**: For fast content delivery across European markets.
- **Multilingual CMS configuration**: English, German, and French content variants.

## Qualitative Impact and Business Transformation

The launch of the new digital infrastructure shifted the company's sales dynamic decisively. Before the rebuild, the website generated a trickle of low-quality inquiries, often missing contact details or vehicle specifics. After launch, the volume of inbound quote requests grew substantially, and the quality improved because the forms captured exactly what a lead was looking for and what they had browsed. Sales representatives started their conversations armed with context, which shortened the qualification time and improved the customer experience.

Paid search campaigns began delivering a steady stream of buyers who were actively in the market, reducing the company's reliance on walk-in traffic and phone calls from outdated contact lists. The cost per qualified lead settled at an efficient level that allowed the marketing spend to be confidently scaled. LinkedIn campaigns brought the brand in front of procurement managers who had never heard of the company, generating high-value conversations that the sales team could nurture over time. Remarketing kept the company's inventory top of mind, with many conversions attributed to a user seeing a truck, leaving the site, and returning weeks later through a display ad.

The social media presence evolved from an afterthought to a genuine asset. The company's LinkedIn following grew consistently, and the engagement on posts shifted from passive likes to meaningful comments and direct inquiries. The sales team reported that prospects increasingly mentioned seeing the company's content before taking a call, a clear signal that the authority-building strategy was working. The automated reporting and scheduling freed the marketing lead to focus on high-impact activities like producing video walkarounds and writing in-depth articles, while the automation ensured the pipeline never ran dry.

Ultimately, the project proved that even in a traditional, relationship-driven industry like commercial vehicle sales, digital execution can become a decisive competitive advantage. A well-architected website, precision-targeted advertising, and a credible social presence do not replace the handshake. They secure it, by ensuring that when a buyer is ready to talk, your company is the one they call first.
