# How Small Businesses Can Use AI Automation Today

When people talk about AI automation, the conversation usually centers on enterprise tools with six-figure price tags and dedicated engineering teams. That narrative misses something important: small businesses stand to gain the most from automation, and the barrier to entry has never been lower.

## Start with Email

If you run a small business, your inbox is probably a mess. Customer inquiries, vendor confirmations, booking requests — they all land in the same place. Tools like Zapier and Make can sort incoming emails by sender or subject, tag them, and route them to the right person. A five-minute setup saves hours every week.

## Customer Support Without the Headcount

You don't need a 24/7 support team anymore. A well-configured chatbot trained on your FAQ page can handle the majority of first-contact questions. If the bot can't resolve the issue, it escalates to a human with the full conversation context attached. Platforms like Tidio and Intercom make this accessible even for solo operators.

## The Spreadsheet Problem

Most small businesses run on spreadsheets — invoices, inventory, client lists. The problem isn't the spreadsheet; it's the manual data entry. AI tools can now extract data from PDFs, emails, and even photos of receipts and populate your sheets automatically. That alone frees up hours per employee per week.

## The Real Bottleneck Isn't Technology

It's the belief that automation is for someone else. The businesses I've seen succeed with AI aren't the ones with the biggest budgets. They're the ones who started small — one workflow, one process — and expanded from there. Pick the task you hate most and automate it first. Everything else follows.
